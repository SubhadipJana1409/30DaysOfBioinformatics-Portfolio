🏆 Day 30 — Capstone COMPLETE
The grand finale — 30 days, 4 blocks, 28+ real datasets, one integrated portfolio. Every project synthesised into a single scientific narrative.
Final Report Available ↓
🔬 Key Integrated Finding
Gut dysbiosis (↓ Faecalibacterium prausnitzii, ↑ Escherichia/Shigella) co-occurs with elevated clinical MDR risk across all analysis blocks, supporting the microbiome–resistome axis as a clinically actionable biomarker.
🧬 Microbiome as AMR Predictor

Gut microbiome composition predicts IBD with AUC=0.968 and co-varies with clinical AMR risk, suggesting microbiome profiling could augment conventional clinical risk scores.

🤖 AMR Classifier Translation

Random Forest AMR classifiers achieved AUC=0.993 (VAN) — performance sufficient for clinical decision support tools in ICU infection management.

📈 CIP Resistance Trajectory

Ciprofloxacin resistance increased 2.25× from 2002–2017, consistent with WHO warnings. Empiric fluoroquinolone therapy should be reviewed in high-risk settings.

⚡ Transfer Learning Wins

Transfer learning achieved 3× better AUC than scratch training with limited labelled data, supporting its application where labelled clinical samples are scarce.

🦠 Phage Therapy Potential

Crassvirales lytic phages targeting Prevotella/Bacteroides documented with high confidence — candidates for phage therapy targeting IBD-associated dysbiosis.

🧫 Real Data Validated

96 DEGs in real GSE71562 E. coli RNA-seq data (flagella/chemotaxis operon). All analyses reproducible with open-source Python 3.10+ libraries.
